# The Store at Five Corners - AI Prompt Reference

## QUICK PROMPT VERSION
"The Store at Five Corners" - a historic two-story cream/pale yellow clapboard colonial building with FOUR tall white columns spanning two stories, supporting an extended porch roof. Dark green shutters and dark green front door. Second-floor balcony with white railings between the two middle columns. Main sign "The STORE AT FIVE CORNERS" in gold text sits in the gable above the columns. Glass-enclosed sunroom addition on left side (mostly windows with white trim) with its own second-floor balcony. Red barn visible across the rural five-way intersection. Tall pine trees and Berkshire mountains in background. Pumpkins displayed on porch in autumn.

## DETAILED BUILDING DESCRIPTION

### Overall Structure
- **Style**: Historic two-story New England colonial building (Est. 1787)
- **Siding**: Cream/pale yellow horizontal clapboard (entire building)
- **Roof**: Light gray peaked roof with triangular gable
- **Trim**: White trim throughout

### The Four Columns (KEY FEATURE)
- **NUMBER**: FOUR white columns (not two)
- **HEIGHT**: Two stories tall - spanning from ground to roofline
- **FUNCTION**: Support an extension of the roof that covers the porch below
- **SPACING**: Evenly spaced across the front facade
- **STYLE**: Classic colonial columns with decorative capitals
- **CREATES**: A grand colonial portico effect

### Main Facade (Cream/Yellow Section)
- **Porch**: Covered porch at ground level, sheltered by the column-supported roof extension
- **Front Door**: DARK GREEN door (matches shutters) at center
- **Second Floor Balcony**: White-railed balcony between the TWO MIDDLE columns only (not spanning all four)
- **Main Sign**: "The STORE AT FIVE CORNERS" in gold/cream text on dark background, located IN THE GABLE above the columns (triangular roof peak area)
- **Secondary Sign**: Below main sign showing services
- **Ground Floor Windows**: Large storefront windows flanking the green door

### Windows & Shutters
- **Shutters**: DARK GREEN shutters on ALL windows (matches front door)
- **Style**: Traditional divided-light windows
- **Ground Floor**: Large storefront display windows
- **Second Floor**: Windows visible between and beside the columns

### Left Side - Sunroom/Café Addition
- **Color**: Same CREAM/PALE YELLOW siding as main building
- **Structure**: Two-story addition connecting to main building
- **Ground Floor**: Glass-enclosed sunroom - MOSTLY WINDOWS with white trim/frames (café seating inside)
- **Second Floor**: Balcony/deck with WHITE RAILINGS
- **Trim**: White trim and window frames throughout
- **Key feature**: The sunroom is predominantly glass/windows, so it appears lighter - any exposed wood is WHITE

### Right Side
- **Small Addition**: Cream-colored small structure with white fence/railing
- **Consistent style**: Matches main building aesthetic

## COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID

- ❌ Only 2 columns (there are FOUR)
- ❌ Columns only one story (they're TWO stories tall)
- ❌ Sign on the building face (it's IN THE GABLE above the columns)
- ❌ No balcony (there IS a second-floor balcony between the two MIDDLE columns)
- ❌ Balcony spanning all four columns (it's only between the TWO MIDDLE columns)
- ❌ Urban setting (it's RURAL, at a five-road intersection)
- ❌ No red barn (the red barn across the street is iconic)
- ❌ Blue or other colored shutters (always DARK GREEN)
- ❌ Non-green front door (door is DARK GREEN, matches shutters)
- ❌ Modern building style (it's historic colonial, Est. 1787)
- ❌ Forgetting the sunroom's second-floor balcony
- ❌ Forgetting that sunroom is mostly WINDOWS with white trim
